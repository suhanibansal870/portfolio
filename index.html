A Machine Learning-Based Diagnostic Utility for Predicting Ertapenem Resistance in Klebsiella Pneumoniae Using MALDI-TOF MS Spectra

Using mass spectrometry data and machine learning to detect antibiotic resistance faster than traditional methods.

  • Published and Presented at IEEE CCWC 2026
  • 1st Place, Biological Sciences, Golden Gate STEM Fair
  • Qualified to California State Science and Engineering Fair
  • Developed a machine learning diagnostic approach to distinguish ertapenem-resistant and susceptible K. pneumoniae isolates using high-dimensional MALDI-TOF MS data
  • Built models achieving 86–99% accuracy
  • Built a complete data preprocessing and analysis pipeline including noise reduction, normalization, spectral alignment, and feature evaluation
  • Applied data balancing techniques (SMOTE, ADASYN, class-weighting, undersampling)
  • Assessed performance using stratified cross-validation, confusion matrices, precision, recall, and F1 score
  • Developed a functional web application for more widespread usage
  • Presented the research to experts in technology and biology

Genetic and Bioinformatics Analysis of Celecoxib: A CYP2C9-Focused Study

Investigating how genetic variants in CYP2C9 affect how the body metabolizes a common pain medication.

  • 3rd Place, Bioinformatics, Alameda County Science and Engineering Fair
  • Designed and conducted an independent pharmacogenomics study examining how CYP2C9 genetic variants influence celecoxib metabolism and treatment risk
  • Analyzed CYP2C9*2 and CYP2C9*3 genetic variants
  • Explored genotype-phenotype relationships relevant to personalized medicine by analyzing sequence data and protein interactions
  • Leveraged multiple bioinformatics tools to perform sequence alignment, predict variant impact, and interpret functional consequences
  • Presented the research to experts in computational biology and bioinformatics
